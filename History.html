Server History

A lot has happened in the time the server's been up!

Hi there! Welcome to Project Nodenium.
This is a super brief overview of our years of server history! If you want to know more about the past seasons feel free to message Camo#0477 on Discord. I'll be happy to answer any questions you have! Enjoy!

Pre-Seasons

2-9 Members - 05/??/2015

First House Ever Built

The server was started back somewhere in May of 2015 by the founder Camo, the exact date is lost to time. It was run off of an old iPhone 4 that sat under a desk fan, far from the setup we have today. The server looked very different back then, nobody could've imagined what it would become. The server was originally called "LPS" (Let's Play Survival) and only consisted of a few friends from school. There were no mega-builds or crazy farms, just some good old-fashioned 2015 survival.

LPS 10 Town Hall

This LPS era of the server lasted for 10 seasons, each being relatively short and uneventful. Nonetheless, it was the start to building what would become the server we know and love today.

Then finally, after years of the server being private, Camo decided to open it up to the public. The first member applied and joined the server on June 29th, 2019 during season 10. This sparked a massive revolution. Almost all the original members, who were all real-life friends of Camo, left the server with the new influx of external members, although, Camo kept the server going and it continued to grow.

Season 1

26 Members - 07/22/2019

Season 1 Nether Hub

And so began the next chapter of the server. We were officially trying to become something. We had big hopes and big ideas but no real direction. We were just a bunch of kids who wanted to play Minecraft together. We had no idea what we were doing and no structure to the mod team or server. Trying to seem more professional, we decided to rename the server to "Hydrocraft". A name that didn't stick for too long.

Season 2

11 Members - 08/24/2019

Swoozies Base

After seeing what it took to run a public server, the whole mod team was completely burnt out after only a month. If they wanted this server to continue, they needed to make some changes. So they did. They restructured the way things were organized and tried bringing the server back to more of a survival feel. And you know what? It completely failed! Absolute disaster. The server was only up for a couple of weeks before not a single person played on it. This completely demoralized the team and the community and we gave up on the dream of having our own server.

Season 3

141 Members - 01/15/2020

Season 3 Original Shopping District

But you wouldn't be reading this right now if we had given up. So what happened? Well in early January 2020 Camo got a message from that very first member to ever join asking if there was going to be another season. Camo was shocked. He hadn't even thought about Minecraft for months but this one message reignited his passion for the game. He decided to give it another shot and started working on pursuing the supposedly impossible goal of making a public SMP work. He started by recruiting a new mod team and reworking the server from the ground up. Giving up the old name of Hydrocraft, a new name, "Nodecraft" was born. Now that's starting to look a little more familiar, isn't it?

Season 3 Revamped Shopping District

This time it was going to be completely different. No more relaxation. If this was going to work we were going to have to take it seriously. We were going to have to work hard. And that's exactly what we did. We worked our asses off. We furiously recruited new members and onboarded a large staff of mods so that there could be at least one person online and active at all times. Everything was monitored through command blocks and we had a strict set of rules that everyone had to follow. We were going to make this work no matter what it took.

Snipe Shots Gold Farm

But of course, working 12-16 hour shifts every day for months is unsustainable. Slowly but surely staff members started to burn out and leave. The server started to turn to anarchy. With such a massive player base and no staff to moderate it: crime became rampant. After 100 days of the season being up, we decided to call it quits. We had tried a small server, but that got boring. We tried a massive server, but that was chaos. We needed to find a happy medium.

Season 4

45 Members - 05/01/2020

Season 4 Shopping District

Season 4 was just that. Our happy medium. This was the first season that we started to figure out what we wanted out of this server and had enough experience with running a server and managing a community to make it work. We still had a relatively large player base but we had a much smaller staff team and a much more relaxed set of rules. We were able to focus on the community and the people who were playing on the server instead of the server itself. This made all the difference. There were still a few issues with griefers and toxic players but we were able to handle them quickly and efficiently. But most importantly, we made the server a genuinely fun place to be. We had almost achieved our goal of making an oasis in the mess that is the Minecraft SMP community.

Ishamabeht and Zebgo's Base

Mega projects were everywhere and the economy was thriving. Life was good. Now that we weren't trying to accept everyone who applied we were able to focus on finding skilled builders and technical players to join. But when the 1.16 Nether Update came out the server experienced some game-breaking bugs that made it impossible to play. It ruined the season for ages and we were forced to shut it down and wait for the bugs to be fixed. It was a very abrupt end to a very fun season.

Season 5

25 Members - 08/10/2020

Season 5 Nether Hub

Season 5 was mostly uneventful despite lasting a fair while for the time. We all worked on our respective projects and worked on honing our building and technical skills.

Season 5 Shopping District

We were getting better and better at the game, building bigger bases, building bigger farms, and creating more lag. But the server hardware wasn't getting bigger at all. Server TPS was abysmal and the server was constantly crashing. If we wanted to keep expanding we were going to have to upgrade the server. But how were we going to do that? We were just a bunch of kids with no money. We had to get creative.

Season 6

28 Members - 12/26/2020

The Original Server Laptop

Camo had an old laptop he set up in his closet with an old fan running across it to keep everything cool. This would be our new server. We looked into trying to get a server from a hosting company but it was just too expensive. We knew we wanted to make this something we were going to be doing long-term so it was worth setting up something decent now.

Season 6 Shopping District

We looked into running a bedrock server, the platform we had been playing on up until this point, but we found out that it was just too limited. We wanted to be able to run plugins and have a lot of control over the server. We decided to switch to Java Edition. It was a difficult transition and a hell of a learning curve but we were able to get everything set up and running in a few weeks. We used Geyser to allow bedrock players to join the server and everything was working great.

The City of Ertwa

Season 6 was quite amazing. This was the next chapter in the server's history. We had some phenomenal builders and everyone was extremely active and excited to be playing on the server. We had a lot of fun and made a lot of progress. But as always, the server eventually died down and we all got burnt out.

Season 7

36 Members - 05/01/2021

Season 7 Shopping District

Season 7 looked promising. We had a lot of new players and a lot of old players coming back. Everyone was super excited to be playing on the server again. But after a few weeks, it was evident that it was not going to last as long as we thought it would. Many players were getting quickly burnt out and leaving.

Camos Custom Island

Throughout the season there had been multiple disputes between players and some bad decisions made that led to some drama. We had all planned some massively ambitious projects for the season but we were all getting burnt out and with activity dwindling we decided to call it a season early.

It wasn't all bad though. We had some amazing builds and some great times. We had a lot of fun and we learned a lot. And even though the season was short because of some drama and bad luck we were able to take a lot away from it to help us do better in the future.

Season 8

32 Members - 06/25/2021

Season 8 Town Hall

In season 8, with the Caves and Cliffs update rapidly approaching, we decided to implement a data pack that would allow us to play the new update early. While this seemed like a great idea, in theory, it ended up being a disaster. The data pack was buggy and caused issues and lag.

8K's Island Base

Once again like in season 7, however, while we had our fun it came to an end quickly. So many of us had planned insanely ambitious projects for the season that soon presented themselves as far more difficult than we had anticipated. Burnout was a huge issue and we were all getting tired of the server. Two bad seasons in a row was not what we wanted. We had to do something different. Ownership of the server was passed to Vyruz, who had been a moderator and a great player for the past few seasons. While we were all excited to see what he would do with the server, we were all still a little sceptical that he would be able to keep the server alive after all the previous failures.

Season 9

32 Members - 08/20/2021

The Vyllage

Now under the leadership of Vyruz, season 9 was a huge success. We had a lot of new players and the strongest community we had ever had. Everyone was having a great time with no burnout for months. Projects were bigger and better than ever. The server was thriving thanks to the way Vyruz had planned it, where we are all in proximity to each other and can easily work together on projects as we all live on a single giant continent.

TJ's Town

It wasn't perfect though, what would be a season without something going wrong? We got contacted by the owner of a company called 'Nodecraft', a server hoster with the same name as us that we had no idea about. They claimed that we were infringing on their trademark and that we had to change our name. We had no other choice but to change our name and branding, which we chose 'Project Nodenium'.

This proved to be a beneficial change for us. We took it as an opportunity to rebrand and restructure the server from a small traditional SMP to a serious building community server with a focus on large-scale projects, the identity we still uphold to this day.

Christmas Event

In the end season, 9 was wonderful. There were great events like karaoke nights and the huge Christmas festival. But most of all we managed to complete some of the biggest projects we had ever attempted. How could we top that?

Season 10

39 Members - 03/19/2022

Season 10 Nether Hub

Spoiler alert: We did!

Taking what we had learned about being close to each other and working together, we decided to take it to the next level. We decided to all live in a small town we built up in a valley called 'The Bowl'. Here we had all our starter bases and shops within only a few seconds walk of each other. We build even more absolutely massive projects such as our glorious nether hub.

Upgraded Server Rig

But we didn't just upgrade our builds. Once again with the huge developments in the size of our builds and our farms, the lag started to become an issue. Hosting the server on that crappy laptop wasn't gonna work much longer. So we decided to upgrade to a dedicated server. With the insanely generous donations from our community, we were able to upgrade our server to an extremely powerful machine that could handle anything we threw at it in just a week. It's amazing what can be achieved when everyone works together.

The Bowl

As we said in season 9, it's going to take a lot to top this season. But with the way we're going, I don't think we'll have any trouble. This is the most incredible community!
